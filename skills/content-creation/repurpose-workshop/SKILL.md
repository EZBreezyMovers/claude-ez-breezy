# Repurpose Workshop

Write once. Publish ten times. The creators who win don't produce more. They distribute smarter.

This skill takes any source material and turns it into channel-native posts. Not lazy cross-posting. Posts that feel like they were built for each platform from scratch.

**The multiplier effect:** One newsletter can become 1 LinkedIn slide deck + 2 LinkedIn written posts + 1 X thread + 3 standalone tweets + 2 Instagram slide decks + 1 Reel script + 2 TikTok scripts + 1 YouTube Short script = 13 pieces from a single source.

---

## what this does

Turn source material into **channel-native posts** that:

| Requirement | Why It Matters |
|-------------|----------------|
| Align with each channel's ranking signals | Your content gets shown to more people |
| Follow format-specific structures | Native content outperforms cross-posts by 40-60% |
| Open with tested scroll-stoppers | The first line/second determines everything |
| Feel built for that channel | Audiences can smell repurposed content |

## source material

### what works as input

**High-yield sources** produce 10+ pieces of content. **Medium-yield sources** produce 5-8.

- **Blog posts** produce content for every channel (high yield, tons of raw material)
- **Newsletters** work best on LinkedIn, X, and Instagram (high yield)
- **Podcast episodes** shine as short video clips, threads, and slide decks (very high yield)
- **Long videos** break into Shorts, Reels, TikToks, and slide decks (very high yield)
- **Webinars and talks** feed every channel (very high yield)
- **Case studies** pair well with LinkedIn and X threads (high yield)
- **Data and research** make strong slide decks, threads, and standalone posts (medium-high yield)
- **Processes and frameworks** translate into slide decks, threads, and video scripts (high yield)

### what to mine from the source

Pull these seven elements from any piece of content:

| Element | What to Look For |
|---------|-----------------|
| Central takeaway | The single idea someone should walk away with |
| Building blocks | 3-7 sub-points that support the central takeaway |
| Concrete illustrations | Stories, examples, anecdotes that bring points to life |
| Proof points | Numbers, stats, results that back up claims |
| Unexpected angles | Opinions that push back on conventional thinking |
| Next steps | What someone can actually do with this information |
| Standalone lines | Sharp phrases that work on their own without context |

## channel blueprints

### LinkedIn

**Ranking Signals (December 2025):**

| Signal | Weight | Details |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Dwell time | Strongest | How long someone spends reading your post |
| Topic authority | Very high | Posting consistently in one niche builds algorithmic trust |
| Golden hour performance | High | First 60 minutes decides reach to 2nd/3rd-degree connections |
| Relevance over recency | High | Mid-2025 update surfaces older posts (2-3 weeks) if they match user interests |
| Authentic engagement | Moderate-high | AI flags engagement pods by analyzing comment velocity and patterns |
| No outbound links | Moderate | Posts without links get meaningfully more distribution |

**Current state:** Organic views dropped roughly 50%, engagement roughly 25% as the platform shifted toward quality over quantity. Stick to 2-3 posts per week, not daily.

**Format guide:**

- **Slide deck:** 5-10 slides at 1080x1350px. Drives the highest dwell time. Save-worthy content wins here.
- **Written post:** 1,200-1,500 characters with a 3-line opening. Depth beats frequency.
- **Document post:** PDF format, 10-15 pages max. Frameworks and guides perform well.
- **Video:** 30-90 seconds, captions mandatory. Lower reach than text or slide decks.

#### LinkedIn slide deck structure

**Opening slide**
Bold claim or question that challenges assumptions. End with "Swipe to learn [specific outcome]"

**Content slides (2-6)**
One numbered point per slide. 2-3 sentences of explanation. Add a visual or example where possible.

**Recap slide**
Five points summarized in five words each. Quick reference for people who swiped fast.

**Close slide**
"Found this useful?" followed by: follow for more [topic], repost to help others, save for later. Your name or handle at the bottom.

#### LinkedIn written post structure

Open with a scroll-stopper (first line must stop the thumb). Add a line break, then 2-3 lines of context on why this matters. Line break. Five numbered points with brief explanations. Line break. The "so what" takeaway. Line break. A question or call to action. Finish with 3-5 hashtags at the bottom, separated by a line.

#### LinkedIn scroll-stoppers

| Type | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Pushback opener | "Stop [thing everyone does]. It's wrecking your [result]." |
| Narrative opener | "Last week, I [did something]. What happened next changed how I think about [topic]." |
| Numbered preview | "[Number] [things] that [outcome]. (Number [X] is the one nobody mentions.)" |
| Authority opener | "After [impressive stat/experience], here's what I know for sure about [topic]:" |
| Curiosity opener | "Why do [surprising thing happen]? I finally figured it out." |
| Proof opener | "[Counterintuitive claim]. Here's the evidence:" |

### Twitter/X

**Ranking Signals (December 2025):**

- **Replies** carry the most weight, especially from people you interact with regularly
- **Quote tweets** deliver 2x the engagement value of a plain retweet
- **Time on tweet** and time spent on linked content both count
- **Profile visits** triggered by curiosity signal quality
- **Media content** (images, video, GIFs) gets a visibility bump
- **First-hour engagement** is critical for amplification

**Current state:** The Following feed now uses Grok AI for ranking based on past behavior and topic interests. No longer chronological. Users default to the For You feed.

**Format guide:**

| Format | Sweet Spot | Performance Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Single tweet | Under 100 characters | Highest engagement rate per impression |
| Thread | 8-15 tweets | Best vehicle for depth and follower growth |
| Quote tweet | Add genuine value to original | 2x engagement vs plain retweet |
| Image tweet | 1200x675px | 35% more engagement than text alone |

#### X thread structure

**Opening tweet:** Bold claim or promise. One-line summary of what they will learn. Thread emoji.

**Body tweets (2 through X):** Numbered point as headline. 2-3 sentences of explanation. Example or proof where it fits.

**Closing tweet:** TL;DR with bullet points summarizing each point. "If this helped: 1. Follow @handle for more. 2. RT the first tweet." Link if relevant.

#### standalone tweet formats

**The Observation:** Counterintuitive insight about your space. "Most people think [X]. But [Y] is actually true because [Z]."

**The Rundown:** [Number] [things] that [outcome], followed by bullet points. Close with "Which one hits different?"

**The Spicy Take:** "Unpopular opinion:" followed by a contrarian statement and one-line reasoning.

**The Invitation:** Provocative question about your space. "Genuine question. Reply with your take."

**The Receipt:** Impressive result or stat, followed by "Here's exactly how:" and 3-5 bullet points.

#### X scroll-stoppers

- **Obituary opener:** "[Thing] is dead. Here's what's replacing it:"
- **Receipts opener:** "I [did X] for [time period]. Here's what happened:"
- **Spicy opener:** "This will piss off [group], but [claim]."
- **Gap opener:** "The [industry] secret no one talks about:"
- **Proof opener:** "[Specific result] in [timeframe]. No [common excuse]. Here's the playbook:"

### Instagram

**Ranking Signals (December 2025):**

| Signal | Weight | Details |
|--------|--------|---------|
| DM shares ("sends per reach") | Strongest | One of the biggest discovery signals, especially for Reels and Explore |
| Saves | Very high | Indicates lasting value. Critical for Feed and Explore |
| Watch time and completion | High | For Reels, completion rate is king |
| Likes per reach | Moderate-high | Quality signal, not raw like count |
| Early velocity | High | First 30-90 minutes decides whether it gets pushed wider |
| Relationship signals | Moderate | DMs, profile taps, comment history with the account |

**Current state:** Photos getting more love in the Feed again (per Adam Mosseri). Slide decks expanded to 20 slides in some regions. Hashtag weighting dropped significantly. New "Your Algorithm" feature shows users why they see content.

**Format guide:**
- **Slide deck:** 6-10 slides (up to 20) at 1080x1350px. Highest engagement. Save magnet.
- **Reel:** 7-15 seconds for viral reach, 30-45 seconds for tutorials. Best discovery tool.
- **Single image:** 1080x1350px. Getting renewed support in 2025.
- **Story:** 1080x1920px, under 15 seconds. Best for DM engagement.

#### Instagram slide deck structure

**Cover slide (the hook):** Bold statement or question. Large, readable font. Minimal design with high contrast.

**Setup slide:** Why this matters, or what most people get wrong.

**Content slides (3-8):** One point per slide. Large text, minimal words. Visual hierarchy with a headline and 1-2 supporting lines.

**Recap slide (optional):** Checkmark list of all points. Quick reference for fast swipers.

**Close slide:** "Save this for later" plus follow and share prompts. Your handle.

#### Instagram caption structure

Hook line that works in the preview (before the "more" cutout). Three dots for spacing. Body with the insight, story, or value in 2-4 short paragraphs. Divider line. Save/share/comment prompts with relevant emoji. Divider line. 3 niche hashtags at the bottom.

#### Instagram Reel script (15-30 seconds)

Seconds 0-3: Pattern interrupt or bold claim that stops the scroll. Seconds 3-20: "Here's [what/why/how]:" followed by three brief points. Seconds 20-30: "Follow for more [topic]" or "Save this for later" or "Send to someone who needs this."

#### Instagram Story sequence

1. **Hook story:** Poll or question sticker. "Quick question..." with two options.
2. **Setup story:** "Here's why I ask..." with brief context.
3. **Value stories (3-5):** One point per story. Text animation or stickers for engagement.
4. **Close story:** "Want the full breakdown?" with a link sticker, or "DM me [word] for [resource]."

### TikTok

**Ranking Signals (December 2025):**

- **First-second retention** drives everything. Completion rate is still number one, but the opening seconds carry disproportionate weight.
- **Rewatch rate** signals strong content. Multiple views = big boost.
- **Shares to DMs** rank higher than public shares.
- **Niche community alignment** now beats broad virality. Specialized audiences get rewarded.
- **Contextual categorization** lets the AI sort humor from education from emotion, matching content to interests.
- **Production quality** matters now. Lighting, sound, and editing factor into ranking.

**Current state:** The algorithm now rewards longer content (30-60+ seconds) when retention stays high. Niche communities (#BookTok, #MarketingTok) get boosted over generic viral attempts. Deeper AI personalization analyzes watch duration, replays, and cross-platform habits.

**Format guide:**

| Format | Length | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Quick hit | 15-30 seconds | Highest completion rates |
| Standard | 30-60 seconds | Now favored when retention holds |
| Deep dive | 1-3 minutes | Works for engaged niche audiences |
| All formats | 1080x1920px (9:16) | Vertical mandatory |

#### TikTok script (15-30 seconds)

**Hook (0-3 seconds):** Visual hook and verbal hook at the same time.

Options: "Stop scrolling if you [identifier]" / "POV: You just realized [insight]" / "The [industry] secret no one tells you:" / "[Controversial statement]. Let me explain." / "I'm about to save you [time/money/pain]"

**Body (3-25 seconds):** "Here's the thing:" followed by three points delivered fast, keeping momentum, building to a payoff.

**Close (25-30 seconds):** "Follow for more [topic]" / "Part 2?" (drives comments) / "Save this" (drives saves)

#### TikTok scroll-stoppers

| Type | How It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Pattern break | Unexpected visual or statement that disrupts the scrolling rhythm |
| Identity callout | "This is for my [specific group] who [specific situation]" |
| Result first | Show the outcome immediately, then explain the path |
| Controversy spark | "I'm going to get hate for this but [take]" |
| Knowledge gap | "I can't believe [industry/brand] doesn't want you to know this" |
| Speed promise | "In 30 seconds I'll show you how to [specific outcome]" |

#### TikTok content structures that perform

1. **Before/After** showing transformation upfront
2. **Green Screen** with you plus content behind you (tweets, articles, data)
3. **Stitch/Duet** reacting to trending content in your niche
4. **Day in the Life** that is niche-specific
5. **POV** with a relatable scenario and a sharp insight
6. **Listicle** with 3 things delivered at speed
7. **Myth Bust** starting with "Stop believing [common misconception]"

### YouTube

**Ranking Signals (December 2025):**

| Signal Level | Signal | Details |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Video | Click-through rate (CTR) | How often people click when your video appears |
| Video | Average view duration + % watched | Both raw watch time and completion percentage |
| Video | Session impact | Whether your video keeps people on YouTube longer |
| Video | Viewer satisfaction | YouTube uses surveys and behavior to estimate quality |
| Video | Negative feedback | "Not interested," skips, very low retention hurt rankings |
| Channel | Topical focus | Channels that stay in a clear lane get recommended more |

**Current state:** AI-driven hyper-personalization accounts for device, time of day, and individual habits. Older evergreen videos get revived when topics trend again. Authority and depth are rewarded, aligned with Google Search updates.

**Format guide:**

- **Shorts:** 10-35 seconds for discovery, up to 60 seconds. Highest reach but shallower engagement.
- **Long-form:** 8-12 minute sweet spot for most niches. Best for monetization and depth.
- **Thumbnails:** 1280x720px. Target 4-10% CTR from Home, 6-12% is strong.

#### YouTube Shorts script

**Hook (0-2 seconds):** Immediate value promise or pattern interrupt. Examples: "Here's why [common belief] is wrong" / "[Number]-second [topic] lesson" / "The [industry] hack that changed everything"

**Body (2-50 seconds):** Deliver value fast. Each point gets 5-10 seconds max. Keep moving visually. Don't stand still.

**Close (50-60 seconds):** "Subscribe for more [topic]" or "Full video on my channel" or end abruptly (drives rewatches for missed content).

#### YouTube long-form structure: The GRABS Method

**G - Grab (0-30 seconds)**
Pattern interrupt or bold claim. Quick credibility if needed. Preview of what they will learn. "By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to [outcome]."

**R - Reveal (30 seconds - 1 minute)**
Brief context on why this matters. Who this is for. What you will cover. "Let's get into it."

**A - Advance (main content)**
Deliver on the promise. Clear sections with verbal signposting. "First... Second... Third..." Examples and proof for each point.

**B - Boost (throughout)**
Every 2-3 minutes, drop an engagement prompt: "Let me know in the comments if [question]" / "Hit like if [relatable statement]" / "If this is clicking, subscribe."

**S - Seal (final 30 seconds)**
Summarize key points. Give a clear next action. "If you want to go deeper on [topic], watch this video next." End screen with subscribe button and related video.

#### YouTube thumbnail and title patterns

**Thumbnail rules:** Maximum 3 elements (face, text, object). High contrast colors. Must be readable at small size. Emotion on face if showing one. Show the outcome, not the process.

**Title formulas:**

- "How I [result]" (example: "How I Built a 100K Newsletter in 8 Months")
- "[Number] [Things] That [Outcome]" (example: "7 LinkedIn Mistakes Killing Your Reach")
- "Why [Thing] Doesn't Work" (example: "Why Your Content Strategy Isn't Working")
- "The [Adjective] [Thing]" (example: "The Boring Marketing Strategy That Actually Works")
- "I [Did X] For [Time]. Here's What Happened" (example: "I Posted Daily for 90 Days. Here's What Happened")
- "[Year] Guide to [Topic]" (example: "2025 Guide to Growing on LinkedIn")
- "[Thing] vs [Thing]" (example: "Threads vs Twitter: Which One Should You Use?")

## the splitter process

### Stage 1: Mine

Pull the raw material from your source content:

| Element | Capture |
|---------|---------|
| Central takeaway | One sentence that nails the main point |
| Building blocks | 1-5 numbered points with brief explanations |
| Concrete illustrations | Stories and examples listed out |
| Proof points | Stats and numbers |
| Standalone lines | Sharp phrases that work without context |
| Unexpected angles | Takes that push back on conventional thinking |

### Stage 2: Match

Pair each content element with its strongest channels and formats:

- **Central takeaway** goes everywhere as single posts and hooks
- **Building blocks (grouped)** work best as LinkedIn/X slide decks and threads
- **Individual building blocks** become standalone posts on any channel
- **Concrete illustrations** shine as Instagram Reels, TikToks, and Stories
- **Proof points** perform on LinkedIn and X as image posts and slide decks
- **Standalone lines** become quote graphics on X and Instagram
- **Unexpected angles** drive single tweets and TikTok video hooks

### Stage 3: Mold

Shape each piece for its destination:

1. **Format** using the channel blueprints above
2. **Scroll-stopper** selected from channel-specific formulas
3. **Length** adjusted to channel norms
4. **Call to action** that fits the channel
5. **Voice** calibrated to the channel (LinkedIn is most formal, TikTok most casual)

### Stage 4: Schedule

**Recommended publishing sequence:**

| Order | Channel + Format | Timing | Reason |
|-------|-----------------|--------|--------|
| First | LinkedIn slide deck | Day 1 | Longest shelf life |
| Second | X thread | Day 1-2 | Good for sparking discussion |
| Third | Instagram slide deck | Day 2-3 | Adapt LinkedIn design with minor tweaks |
| Fourth | TikTok / Reel | Day 3-4 | Needs video production time |
| Fifth | YouTube Short | Day 4-5 | Can adapt TikTok content |
| Ongoing | Standalone posts | Spread out | Extract individual points over time |

## traps to dodge

### what kills performance

1. **Copy-pasting across channels.** Every channel has different norms. Cross-posted content performs 40-60% worse.
2. **Recycling the same scroll-stopper everywhere.** LinkedIn openers and TikTok openers need completely different energy and format.
3. **Skipping channel-native features.** No hashtags on LinkedIn slide decks. Always add captions on video. Instagram demands visual-first thinking.
4. **Dumping everything at once.** Stagger across days and weeks. Each piece needs room to perform.
5. **Leaving off the call to action.** Every channel piece needs a clear next step, but make it channel-appropriate.

### what drives results

1. Open with the strongest scroll-stopper for that specific channel
2. Trim or expand length to match channel expectations
3. Use native formatting (threads, slide decks, Stories, etc.)
4. Put the best material first, especially in video
5. Build channel-specific visuals when you can

## the quality check

Good repurposing passes six tests:

| Test | What It Means |
|------|--------------|
| Standalone | Each piece makes sense without the source |
| Native | It doesn't feel "repurposed" |
| Right hook | The scroll-stopper matches the channel's energy |
| Front-loaded | Best material comes first |
| Clear next step | Call to action fits the channel |
| Quality over quantity | 5 strong pieces beat 15 mediocre ones |
