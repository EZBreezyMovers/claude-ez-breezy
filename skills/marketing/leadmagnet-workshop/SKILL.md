# Lead Magnet Generator

Your prospect doesn't care about your freebie. They care about the problem eating at them right now. The best lead magnets solve that problem fast, and leave them wanting the full solution you sell.

This skill builds lead magnet concepts that actually pull their weight.

---

## what this produces

When someone asks for lead magnet ideas, the goal is to deliver **several strong concepts** they can pick from. Each concept comes with a clear angle, format choice, and connection to their paid product.

Every business has multiple valid approaches. The job is finding which one fits their audience, model, and offer best.

**Deliverable: 3-5 distinct lead magnet concepts**, each containing:

| Element | What it covers |
|---------|---------------|
| Concept | What the lead magnet is, in one sentence |
| Format | Quiz, PDF, calculator, challenge, template, etc. |
| Angle | Why someone would hand over their email for this |
| Path to paid | How consuming this naturally leads to wanting the full offer |
| Build notes | Difficulty level and resources required |

## gather context before generating

### first: identify the business model

Optimal formats shift depending on what they sell:

**Courses, memberships, coaching:**
- Quizzes and self-assessments perform extremely well
- Multi-day challenges (5-day, 7-day) create momentum and community
- PDF frameworks targeting one narrow problem
- Short video series previewing teaching style
- Free chapters or modules as a sample

**Software and tools:**
- Free utilities or stripped-down product versions
- ROI calculators putting a number on the value
- Templates designed to work alongside the product
- Checklists and setup guides
- Free trials serving the same function

**Agencies, consultants, freelancers:**
- Audits surfacing problems the service fixes
- Diagnostic assessments mapping the prospect's situation
- Case studies proving what you can deliver
- Strategy calls or consultations
- Templates revealing your methodology

### second: identify the transformation

Not the product. The change in the customer's life.

What does their world look like AFTER? Which pain vanishes? What new ability appears? What shifts in how they see themselves?

The lead magnet needs to deliver a BITE-SIZED version of that same shift.

### third: identify the prospect

- Where are they right now?
- What have they already tried that didn't work?
- What do they currently believe about the problem?
- What would make them think "this is exactly what I was looking for"?

## the three rules

### the precision rule

**Narrow crushes broad. No exceptions.**

"The 3-Step System to Stop Your Dog from Pulling on Leash in One Weekend (Even If You've Tried Everything)" massively outperforms "Dog Training Tips."

Precision signals three things:
1. This was built for someone in my exact situation
2. The creator genuinely understands what I'm dealing with
3. This isn't recycled advice available on page one of Google

When building concepts, always sharpen:
- Specific outcome (not "train your dog" but "stop leash pulling in 48 hours")
- Specific timeframe (not "eventually" but "this weekend")
- Specific audience (not "pet owners" but "first-time puppy parents over 40")
- Specific method (not "training tips" but "The Calm Walk Protocol")

### the connection rule

**The lead magnet must link logically to the paid offer.**

If someone downloads a lead magnet about cat behavior and you sell dog training, there's no connection. You attracted people interested in the wrong thing.

The strongest lead magnets function as "Step 1" of what you sell:
- Dog training program → Lead magnet: "The First 3 Commands" (the foundation taught in week one)
- Marketing agency → Lead magnet: Free mini-audit (preview of what the full audit uncovers)
- Productivity coach → Lead magnet: "The Morning Reset Worksheet" (taste of the coaching method)

The connection should be obvious: "If this free piece helped, the paid version goes deeper and further."

### the instant payoff rule

**Fix one specific problem completely.**

Prospects want value they can use RIGHT NOW. A lead magnet requiring weeks of study before anything happens feels like homework, not a gift.

Strong lead magnets produce immediate results:
- A checklist completable in 10 minutes that exposes gaps
- A template customizable in an hour for their situation
- An assessment delivering a score and action steps on the spot
- A calculator showing their personal numbers immediately

Instant results trigger reciprocity. When someone thinks "I couldn't have built this on my own," they're ready to value your paid offer.

### the worth formula

Rate every lead magnet concept on four dimensions:

**Worth = (Desired Result x Believability) / (Wait Time x Effort Required)**

Push up:
- **Desired Result** — What transformation does this promise?
- **Believability** — Why will THIS work when past attempts failed?

Push down:
- **Wait Time** — How soon do they see results? (Now beats next month)
- **Effort Required** — How easy to consume and act on? (5-minute checklist beats 50-page manual)

## choosing the right format

**Quizzes/Assessments**
- Strongest for: Personalization, audience segmentation, transformation-based offers
- Example angles: "What's Your Training Style?", "Find Your Dog's Behavior Type"
- Converts because: People love learning about themselves; you get segmentation data
- Build effort: Medium (needs quiz tool and branching logic)

**PDF Guides/Frameworks**
- Strongest for: Building authority, tackling complex topics, demonstrating depth
- Example angles: "The 7-Step Socialization Blueprint", "Puppy's First 30 Days Framework"
- Converts because: Feels high-value, simple to produce, works for any business type
- Build effort: Low (content plus design)

**Checklists/Templates**
- Strongest for: Immediate use, quick wins, showcasing your method
- Example angles: "New Puppy Prep Checklist", "Weekly Training Schedule Template"
- Converts because: Instantly actionable, minimal friction
- Build effort: Low

**Calculators/Tools**
- Strongest for: Software businesses, financial services, ROI-driven offers
- Example angles: "Cost of Bad Behavior Calculator", "Training Timeline Estimator"
- Converts because: Personalized output, tangible value demonstrated
- Build effort: Medium-High (needs development)

**Challenges (5-day, 7-day)**
- Strongest for: Community building, transformation offers, coaching businesses
- Example angles: "5-Day Calm Dog Challenge", "7-Day Leash Manners Sprint"
- Converts because: Drives engagement, builds habits, shows results in action
- Build effort: Medium (needs email sequence, possibly community space)

**Video Series/Mini-Courses**
- Strongest for: Demonstrating teaching style, complex subjects, premium offers
- Example angles: "3-Part Puppy Training Fundamentals", "Free Behavior Masterclass"
- Converts because: Builds relationship, showcases expertise deeply
- Build effort: Medium (needs video production)

**Free Audits/Assessments**
- Strongest for: Service businesses, agencies, consultants
- Example angles: "Free Dog Behavior Assessment", "Training Plan Review"
- Converts because: Surfaces problems you solve, proves capability
- Build effort: Medium (time investment per lead)

**Swipe Files/Resource Collections**
- Strongest for: Creative industries, marketing, education
- Example angles: "50 Commands Every Dog Should Know", "The Complete Treat & Tool Guide"
- Converts because: Massive perceived value, saves hours of research
- Build effort: Low

## angle builders

Every lead magnet needs an angle strong enough to earn an email address.

| Angle type | Formula | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| The Shortcut | "Get [outcome] without [usual pain/time]" | "Stop Barking in 3 Days Without Yelling or Shock Collars" |
| The Hidden Truth | "The [overlooked thing] that [impressive result]" | "The Socialization Mistake That Creates Aggressive Dogs" |
| The Named Method | "The [method name] for [specific outcome]" | "The Calm Walk Protocol: Loose Leash Walking in One Weekend" |
| The Number | "[X] [things] to [outcome]" | "7 Commands That Prevent 90% of Behavior Problems" |
| The Diagnostic | "Discover your [type/score/level]" | "What's Your Dog's Anxiety Score? Take the 2-Minute Assessment" |
| The Before/After | "How to go from [painful now] to [desired then]" | "From Reactive Mess to Calm Companion: The 30-Day Roadmap" |
| The Proof | "How [person/company] achieved [specific result]" | "How a 'Hopeless' Rescue Dog Passed CGC in 8 Weeks" |

## worked example: online dog training program

### context
- **Product:** $497 online dog training course for new puppy owners
- **Transformation:** Go from overwhelmed first-time owner to confident handler with a well-behaved dog
- **Audience:** First-time puppy parents (25-45) who feel in over their heads
- **Business type:** Info product / online course

### lead magnet options

**Concept 1: The Puppy Survival Checklist**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Concept | Complete checklist covering the first 30 days with a new puppy, organized by week |
| Format | PDF checklist |
| Angle | "Your Puppy's First 30 Days: The Week-by-Week Survival Checklist" |
| Path to paid | The checklist mirrors the course structure; each week's tasks preview what the full course teaches in depth |
| Build notes | Low difficulty; write and design in Canva |

**Concept 2: The Behavior Type Quiz**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Concept | 8-question assessment that identifies the puppy's temperament type and recommended training approach |
| Format | Interactive quiz |
| Angle | "What's Your Puppy's Personality Type? Take the 2-Minute Quiz to Find Out" |
| Path to paid | Quiz results reveal specific challenges for their type; course has modules addressing each type |
| Build notes | Medium difficulty; needs Typeform or ScoreApp setup with branching logic |

**Concept 3: The 3 Commands Framework**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Concept | Step-by-step PDF teaching the three foundational commands every puppy needs first |
| Format | PDF framework |
| Angle | "The Only 3 Commands Your Puppy Needs This Week (Master These Before Anything Else)" |
| Path to paid | These three commands are taught in Module 1; framework proves the teaching method works, full course covers 20+ commands and behavior modification |
| Build notes | Low difficulty; write and design |

**Concept 4: The 5-Day Puppy Reset Challenge**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Concept | Daily email challenge with one training exercise per day, building from simple to intermediate |
| Format | Email challenge |
| Angle | "5 Days to a Calmer Puppy: The Free Training Challenge for New Owners" |
| Path to paid | Challenge demonstrates teaching style, delivers visible results, final email offers course at discount |
| Build notes | Medium difficulty; needs 5 emails plus daily training videos or instructions |

**Concept 5: The Cost of Skipping Training Calculator**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Concept | Interactive tool showing what untrained dog behavior costs over the dog's lifetime (vet bills from ingestion, furniture damage, dog walker fees, boarding rejection costs) |
| Format | Calculator/spreadsheet |
| Angle | "The Hidden Cost of an Untrained Dog: Calculate What You'll Spend Without Proper Training" |
| Path to paid | Calculator reveals the cost is far more than the course price; creates urgency through loss framing |
| Build notes | Medium difficulty; needs spreadsheet or simple web tool |

**Recommended first move:** Concept 1 (Puppy Survival Checklist) for fastest launch with high perceived value, or Concept 2 (Behavior Type Quiz) if segmentation and personalized email follow-up is the priority.

## when this skill gets called

This skill activates when:
- Someone asks "what lead magnet should I create for X"
- Someone asks "how do I grow my email list"
- Someone needs "top of funnel ideas" or "freebie ideas"
- Someone asks "what should I give away for free"
- Someone needs to build an audience before launching
- The landing page skill needs a lead magnet offer to reference
- The email sequence skill needs a lead magnet to deliver

When another skill requires a lead magnet, this skill provides the concept that shapes downstream work.

---

## what this skill does NOT do

This skill generates lead magnet CONCEPTS. It does not:
- Write the actual lead magnet content (separate task)
- Build the opt-in page (that's the landing page skill)
- Write the follow-up email sequence (that's the email sequence skill)
- Design visual assets (that's design work)

The output is strategic direction on WHAT to build, not the build itself.

---

## quality check

Before delivering concepts, verify each one passes:

1. **Precision** — Is the promise sharp and specific? Vague lead magnets ("Marketing Tips") fail.
2. **Complete quick win** — Does it solve one problem fully? Not a teaser, but genuine value.
3. **Clear connection** — Can you trace a straight line from consuming this to wanting the paid offer?
4. **Real desire** — Would the target audience actually want this right now? Not "should want." WANT.
5. **Buildable** — Does the implementation difficulty match available resources?

If any concept fails two or more checks, rework it before including it.
