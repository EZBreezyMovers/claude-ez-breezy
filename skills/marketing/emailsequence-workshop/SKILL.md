# Email Sequence Builder

Subscribers are cheap. Customers are expensive. The gap between "signed up" and "paid" is a graveyard of abandoned inboxes and ignored autoresponders. Most people either blast sales emails too early or send so much free content they never ask for the sale.

This skill builds the path from opt-in to purchase — step by step, email by email.

---

## what this produces

A **complete, ready-to-load email sequence** that moves someone from new subscriber to paying customer without torching the relationship along the way.

Every sequence includes subject lines, preview text, full written copy, send timing, and calls to action.

---

## sequence catalog

**Welcome Path** — Delivers the freebie, builds rapport, plants offer seeds. 5-7 emails after someone opts in.

**Trust Builder** — Pure value emails that deepen the relationship. 4-6 emails between welcome and any sales push.

**Sales Run** — Pitches the paid product directly. 4-7 emails when the relationship is warm enough.

**Campaign Blitz** — Time-boxed promotion with a hard deadline. 6-10 emails for launches, cohort opens, or limited offers.

**Win-Back Series** — Wakes up subscribers who've gone cold. 3-4 emails for anyone inactive 30+ days.

**Buyer Onboarding** — Reduces refunds and sets up upsells. 4-6 emails after purchase.

## before you start: required inputs

**STOP. Do not write a single email until all six inputs below are confirmed with the user. Ask for anything missing.**

| Input | Question to ask | Why it matters |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **Freebie** | What did they sign up for? | Shapes email 1 and the value promise |
| **Paid offer** | What are you selling them? | Determines the pitch and bridge |
| **Price** | How much does it cost? | Higher price = more trust-building needed first |
| **The bridge** | How does the freebie connect to the paid thing? | Makes the pitch feel natural, not random |
| **Voice** | How should these emails sound? | Run brand-voice skill first if undefined |
| **Blockers** | Why might they NOT buy? | Shapes objection-handling emails |

## the welcome path (5-7 emails)

First impressions set the trajectory. This sequence is the foundation everything else builds on.

### what it accomplishes

Hands over the freebie, sets expectations for future emails, starts the relationship, identifies who's engaged, and quietly lays groundwork for the paid offer.

### the progression: Hand Off → Bond → Teach → Teach → Gap → Gentle Ask → Direct Ask

For the full breakdown of every email in this sequence (structures, subject line formulas, and complete examples), see [references/welcome-path.md](references/welcome-path.md).

**Quick summary:**

| Email | Label | Job | Timing |
|-------|-------|-----|--------|
| 1 | Hand Off | Deliver the freebie + one quick action step | Immediately |
| 2 | Bond | Share a personal story that builds rapport | Day 2 |
| 3 | Teach | Give a useful lesson — create a quick win | Day 4 |
| 4 | Teach Again | Different topic, same value. Builds authority | Day 6 |
| 5 | Gap | Show what's possible beyond the freebie | Day 8 |
| 6 | Gentle Ask | Introduce the offer, let them self-select | Day 10 |
| 7 | Direct Ask | Make the clear pitch. One CTA. | Day 12 |

## the sales run (4-7 emails)

Use this when the relationship is warm — either after the welcome path or as its own campaign.

### the progression: Announce → Want → Evidence → Doubt → Deadline → Final Push

| Email | Label | Job |
|-------|-------|-----|
| 1 | Announce | Present the offer and its core promise |
| 2 | Want | Paint the transformation, show the gap between now and after |
| 3 | Evidence | Testimonials, results, case studies |
| 4 | Doubt | Handle the biggest objection head-on |
| 5 | Deadline | Explain why acting now matters (only if real) |
| 6 | Final Push | Last CTA, clear and direct |
| 7 | Closing Bell | Deadline reminder — only if there's an actual deadline |

**Pacing:** Every 2 days normally. Daily during launches. Final 3 emails in 3 days if there's a deadline.

## the campaign blitz (6-10 emails)

For launches, cohort opens, promotions — anything with a hard close date.

### the progression: Tease → Announce → Prove → Handle → Close

**Pre-launch (1-2 emails):** Build anticipation. Hint that something's coming without revealing details.

**Doors open (2-3 emails):** Full announcement with details. Deep-dive on the transformation. Social proof and testimonials.

**Mid-campaign (2-3 emails):** Objection handling. A case study or story. "Is this right for me?" email.

**Doors close (2-3 emails):** 48-hour warning. Final testimonial. Last call on deadline day.

### campaign timeline

| Day | Email | Notes |
|-----|-------|-------|
| -3 | Tease | Optional. Build curiosity |
| -1 | Tomorrow | "Something's coming" |
| 0 (AM) | Doors open | Full announcement |
| 0 (PM) | Different angle | Same offer, different framing |
| 2 | Deep-dive | Transformation and value |
| 4 | Proof | Social proof and results |
| 5 | Objections | Address the "but..." |
| 6 | 48 hours | Urgency kicks in |
| 7 (AM) | 24 hours | Morning reminder |
| 7 (PM) | Final hours | Evening push |
| 7 (late) | Last call | Midnight deadline |

## the win-back series (3-4 emails)

For people who stopped opening. 30+ days of silence.

### the progression: Surprise → Give → Ask → Goodbye

| Email | Label | Job |
|-------|-------|-----|
| 1 | Surprise | Break the pattern. Acknowledge the silence. Different subject line style than usual |
| 2 | Give | Best content you've got. Pure value. No ask |
| 3 | Ask | Direct question: do you still want these emails? |
| 4 | Goodbye | Removing them from the list. Creates real urgency |

**Subject line ideas for win-backs:**
- "Did I lose you?"
- "Want me to stop emailing?"
- "This is awkward"
- "You're about to miss [specific thing]"
- "[First name], still around?"

## writing rules for every email

For subject line formulas, open-rate tactics, and detailed copy principles, see [references/email-craft.md](references/email-craft.md).

**The essentials:**

**P.S. lines are high-value territory.** Nearly half of readers scan the P.S. before the body. Put your CTA, a second hook, a personal note, or a deadline reminder there.

**One action per email.** Multiple CTAs cancel each other out. Pick the one thing you want them to do.

**Short paragraphs only.** 1-3 sentences. People scan email. They don't read it top to bottom.

**Preview text is your second headline.** First 40-90 characters show in the inbox. Don't waste them on "View in browser."

**Curiosity loops keep them coming back.** Drop a teaser for tomorrow's email: "I'll show you what happened next" or "Tomorrow: the part nobody talks about."

**Specifics beat generalities every time.** Not "made money" but "$47,329 in one launch." Not "recently" but "last Thursday." Numbers and dates make claims believable.

## sequence wiring patterns

**Linear** — Email 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → Pitch. Straightforward. No branches. Works for simple sequences.

**Behavioral split** — Email 1 → 2 → Did they click? Yes → pitch track. No → more value track. Smarter but needs automation tools.

**Full lifecycle** — Welcome path (5 emails) → 7-day pause → Sales run (5 emails) → No purchase → Trust builder (ongoing). The complete subscriber journey.

## timing guidelines

### how often to email by sequence type

| Sequence | Frequency | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| Welcome path | Days 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 | Value-heavy upfront while attention is high |
| Trust builder | 1-2x per week | Steady rhythm without fatigue |
| Sales run | Every 2 days | Enough contact without being annoying |
| Campaign blitz | Daily or every other day | Deadline justifies the intensity |
| Win-back | Days 0, 3, 7, 10 | Space to respond between each |

### when to send

- **B2B:** Tuesday through Thursday, 9-11am in recipient's timezone
- **B2C:** Tuesday through Thursday, 7-9am or 7-9pm
- **Skip:** Monday morning (inbox chaos), Friday afternoon (mentally gone)

### how much trust before you sell

| Price range | Value emails first |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Under $100 | 3-5 |
| $100-500 | 5-7 |
| Over $500 | 7-10 (or a sales call) |

Trust required goes up with price. Don't rush the pitch.

## quality check

A strong email sequence:

1. **Earns before it asks** — Minimum 3-5 value emails before any pitch
2. **Every email has a single job** — One purpose, one CTA, no wandering
3. **Reads like a person wrote it** — Not corporate, not guru-speak, not AI
4. **Builds pull toward the next email** — Each one makes them want to open tomorrow
5. **Tackles objections before they form** — Addresses the "but..." preemptively
6. **Drives one action** — Every email points to exactly one thing to do
7. **Treats the reader like an adult** — Easy to unsubscribe, no manipulation

If the sequence reads like "free stuff, free stuff, free stuff, BUY NOW BUY NOW" — it failed. The pitch should feel like the natural next step, not a bait-and-switch.
