### Main Headline: The Four Pieces

**Piece 1: Where they are now (the painful current state)**
- "clueless about copy"
- "stuck at $5k months"
- "posting content that nobody sees"
- "confused by all the AI hype"
- "struggling to get your first client"
- "working 60 hours a week"
- "terrified of selling"

**Piece 2: Where they want to be (the desired outcome)**
- "writing high-converting sales pages"
- "hitting consistent $20k months"
- "building an audience that actually buys"
- "using AI to 10x your output"
- "fully booked 3 months in advance"
- "working 4-day weeks"
- "closing deals without feeling sleazy"

**Piece 3: How fast (specific timeframe)**
- "in less than 2 hours"
- "in the next 7 days"
- "within 30 days"
- "in one afternoon"
- "by next Friday"
- "in a single weekend"

**Piece 4: Handle the knee-jerk objection**

Use "without" for external obstacles:
- "without spending money on ads"
- "without a huge audience"
- "without hiring a team"
- "without writing a single line of code"
- "without quitting your day job"

Use "even if" for internal limiting beliefs:
- "even if you can barely string a sentence together"
- "even if you've failed before"
- "even if you're starting from zero"
- "even if you hate technology"
- "even if you're an introvert who hates selling"
- "even if you've tried everything else"

## Lead Examples

### Lead Formula Step Examples

**Step 1: Call them out**
- "If you're a service professional struggling to find clients..."
- "If you've been posting content for months with nothing to show for it..."
- "If you keep hearing about AI but can't figure out how to actually use it..."
- "If you're a freelancer tired of the feast-or-famine cycle..."
- "If you've tried to learn Claude Code but got stuck at the setup..."

**Step 2: Make them a promise**
- "Then I'm about to share how you can get an endless stream of qualified leads..."
- "I'm going to show you exactly how to build an audience that actually buys..."
- "You're about to discover the simplest way to use AI for your marketing..."
- "I'm going to hand you the system that took me from $3k months to $15k months..."

**Step 3: Within a timeframe**
- "...in the next 7 days (or even sooner)."
- "...within 30 days of implementing what I'm about to show you."
- "...by this time next week."
- "...in a single afternoon."

**Step 4: How it works (brief mechanism)**
- "Using a brand new, little-known traffic source that's completely free and works for any type of service business."
- "Using a simple 3-part content system that takes 30 minutes a day."
- "Using the exact same setup and workflows I use to create 10x more content in half the time."

**Step 5: Handle objections**
- "And this will get you clients even if you're brand new and don't have any testimonials."
- "This works whether you have 100 followers or 100,000."
- "You don't need any technical skills. If you can follow simple instructions, you can do this."

**Step 6: Let them know it's easy and/or quick**
- "It's almost entirely automated and takes less than an hour a day."
- "The whole setup takes 20 minutes. I'll walk you through every click."
- "No complicated tech. No confusing dashboards. Just follow the steps."

**Step 7: Add proof**
- "Plus, it's already helped over 370 professionals get fully booked."
- "This is the same system that's generated $2.3M in sales for my business."
- "I've taught this to 3,000+ students and watched it work in dozens of different industries."

**Step 8: Transition out**
- "Though first, let me introduce myself..."
- "But before I explain how it all works, I need to tell you a quick story..."
- "Let me explain how I stumbled onto this..."

### Problem Examples (Tried and Failed)

**Teaching Copywriting:**
```
Though when it came to teaching others how to write winning copy I struggled.

No matter how well I explained the principles, my students couldn't seem to write it for themselves. Their copy never felt right.

So I had them handcopy classic sales pages... but they got bored, their hands cramped, and they remembered little of what they copied.

I bought them all the usual copywriting books... but they were outdated with no information on how to write modern copy.

I even invested in copywriting courses for them... but they just taught the basics with no insight into the structure of an effective sales page.

And I realised I was wasting their time and my money. If they were going to learn copywriting properly, I would have to be the one to teach them.
```

**Learning AI/Claude Code:**
```
Last year I kept seeing people talk about Claude Code on Twitter. Everyone seemed to be using it. Building things. Automating workflows. Creating content faster.

So I tried to learn it.

I watched YouTube tutorials... but they were either too technical or too basic. I'd follow along, get stuck, and have no idea why.

I read the documentation... but it assumed you already knew things I didn't know. Every answer led to three more questions.

I even paid for a course... but it was made for developers. Within 20 minutes I was lost.

I felt like everyone else got it except me. Like there was some secret handshake I was missing.

Then I realized: there was no guide for people like me. Non-technical people who just wanted to use this thing for their actual work.
```

**Getting Clients:**
```
When I started freelancing, I had no idea how to get clients.

I tried cold emailing... but nobody replied. I must have sent 200 emails that first month. Got maybe 3 responses. Zero clients.

I tried posting on social media... but I had 47 followers. My posts got 2 likes. Both from my mom.

I even tried freelance platforms... but I was competing with people who charged $5/hour. I couldn't win on price and didn't know how to win on anything else.

After 3 months, I'd made exactly $800. I was burning through my savings. I was about to give up and get a "real job."

That's when I stumbled onto something that changed everything.
```

**Pricing Services:**
```
For years I charged $50/hour. I told myself that was "good money."

Then a client mentioned what they paid their previous freelancer. It was 4x my rate. For the same work.

I felt sick. How much money had I left on the table?

So I tried raising my rates... but I chickened out at the last second. I was terrified of losing clients.

I tried adding "premium packages"... but clients just picked the cheap option and asked for the premium deliverables anyway.

I even tried a price increase email... but I spent 3 weeks writing it, never sent it, and went back to charging $50/hour.

I was trapped. I knew I was undercharging, but I didn't know how to charge more without losing everything I'd built.
```

### Finding the Solution Examples

**Teaching Copywriting:**
```
So I started by breaking down my own copywriting process.

I didn't think about the fundamentals or the principles. I just focused on what I actually did, step by step, every time I wrote a sales page.

I made a framework out of it. I had my students follow it. And... finally they got it.

They started creating high-converting copy. They might not have understood exactly what they were doing yet - that can come later - but the results were amazing.

And soon word spread. Other people started asking if they could get access to my framework.

Even the world-famous creator Kieran Drew asked me if I would teach it to his audience.

So I realised I needed to polish it up and package it up into something I could proudly share with other people.
```

**Learning AI/Claude Code:**
```
So I decided to figure it out myself. The hard way.

I spent 3 months trying everything. Breaking things. Getting frustrated. Slowly piecing together what actually worked.

I documented every step. Every mistake. Every "aha" moment. I wrote down exactly what I wished someone had told me on day one.

Eventually, it clicked. I wasn't just using Claude Code - I was using it for hours every day. Writing content. Building workflows. Doing things that used to take me all week in a single afternoon.

Friends started asking how I did it. "Can you just show me your setup?" Colleagues wanted to know what workflows I was using.

So I put together a simple walkthrough. Just the basics. Just enough to get someone from zero to actually using the thing.

And that's what became this workshop.
```

**Getting Clients:**
```
So I started paying attention to freelancers who were fully booked.

Not the ones with huge audiences. The ones with small followings who still had more work than they could handle. What were they doing differently?

I noticed a pattern. They weren't doing more. They were doing something specific that everyone else ignored.

I tested it. Within 3 weeks I had more leads than I'd had in the previous 3 months combined.

I refined the system. Documented it. Turned it into a repeatable process.

By month 6 I was fully booked. By month 12 I had a waitlist.

Other freelancers started asking what I was doing. I showed a few of them. They got results too.

That's when I realized this wasn't just something that worked for me. This was a system that worked.
```
